Vignesh Subramanian

I am a first-year Ph.D. student and a Graduate Research Assistant at the Georgia Institute of Technology under the guidance of Dr. Suguman Bansal in the field of Formal Methods and Reinforcement Learning. My research is centered around the fusion of Formal Methods and Reinforcement Learning techniques with the overarching goal of creating advanced AI systems that can enhance the scalability and efficiency of Reinforcement Learning methods. In essence, my work aims to bridge the gap between Formal Methods, which provide rigorous mathematical guarantees, and Reinforcement Learning, which excels in learning from interactions with the environment. By integrating these two domains, I aspire to develop AI solutions that are not only robust and reliable but also possess the capability to generalize across unseen instances during the training period, ultimately contributing to the scalability and efficiency of the algorithm.

During my undergraduate studies, I interned at Newcastle University in partnership with the Max Planck Institute, working under the supervision of Dr. Sadegh Soudjani and Dr. Arabinda Ghosh to develop Reinforcement Learning solutions for addressing challenges associated with stochastic max-plus linear systems.

    Projects

    TSONHMEA

    The Tri-Stage Occlusion Handling Normal Map Estimation Algorithm is tailored for generating accurate 3D Normal Maps from heavily occluded 2D images. It operates in three stages: the initial phases tackle various occlusions like background, shadow, and blur. In the final stage, the algorithm performs normal map estimation, combining depth and orientation data. This approach ensures robust handling of complex occlusion scenarios and consistently improves the quality of normal map generation across a diverse range of image conditions.

    Samsung Research

    Model Quantization For Diabetes Classification in Edge Devices

    A static quantization approach is employed to optimize AlexNet, MobileNet, and ResNet-50 models trained on the Retina-based Diabetes Classification dataset. This technique aims to achieve superior classification accuracy while substantially reducing the model size. By implementing static quantization without compromising performance, we enhance the overall classification efficiency, allowing for more streamlined and effective model deployment in practical applications.

    IIIT Hyderabad

    Binary Sequence Classification using BERT

    BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) in PyTorch was utilized to solve a binary sequence classification task. The objective was to distinguish paragraphs based on their ability to remain relevant over time, categorizing them as either "evergreen" or "ephemeral." This approach leverages pre-trained word embeddings and transformers to achieve accurate classification.

    Kaggle

    GISiL

    The project is centered around the development of a mechanical exoskeleton with the capability to translate American Sign Language (ASL) into English. Scripts were devised to collect data from mini-potentiometers integrated into the exoskeleton, facilitating the precise measurement of bending angles in each finger joint. Furthermore, a model was constructed within a mobile server using a deep learning network tailored for the classification of ASL alphabet signs based on the sensor outputs.
