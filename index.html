About me

I am a 4th-year Ph.D. student and a Graduate Research Assistant at the Georgia Institute of Technology under the guidance of Dr. Suguman Bansal. My work sits at the intersection of Formal Methods and Reinforcement Learning, with growing interest in how LLMs can enhance decision-making, planning, and generalization in interactive learning environments.

The overarching goal of my research is to create advanced AI systems that improve the scalability, robustness, and trustworthiness of RL algorithms. I aim to bridge Formal Methods, which provide rigorous mathematical guarantees, and Reinforcement Learning, which excels in learning from interaction.

During my undergraduate studies, I interned at Newcastle University in partnership with the Max Planck Institute, working under the supervision of Dr. Sadegh Soudjani to develop Reinforcement Learning solutions for stochastic max-plus linear systems.

Research Interests

My research is concerned with reliable learning-enabled systems: agents that learn, generalize, and still satisfy formally specified behavior.

  1. Formal methods for reinforcement learning and specification-guided generalization.
  2. Verification, certification, and evaluation of learned policies.
  3. LLM-assisted planning, decision-making, and trustworthy autonomous systems.
